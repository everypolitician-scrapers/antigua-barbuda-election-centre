PROFILE

Affectionately known across Antigua & Barbuda as 'Brother B,' Hilson Baptiste is a true man of the people. He is a grassroots man who is regularly out in the community sponsoring events and helping solve problems for his constituents.

He is a product of St. Mary's. His mother was a farmer and housewife. He was one of eleven children. He lost his mother just a year ago. He was the first elected member born in the constituency to represent the constituency. He attended the Bolans Primary and Secondary schools prior to relocating to the United States Virgin Islands (USVI) where he attended St. Thomas High School.

He received culinary training and previously worked as a chef. Becaus eof his interestin the art of cooking, he was chosen to attend a training programme in Washington, DC at the prestigious Culinary Arts School. Upon returning to St. Thomas, he worked hard to put himself through school and make a better life for him and his family.

Brother B returned to Antigua in 1970 and worked at several restaurants. In 1972, he opened his own restaurant, Brother B's, which he operated for many years prior to entering service to his community as an elected official. Brother B's was a community spot where the food was good, there was plenty of community and everyone was welcome.

In Government, Minister Baptiste has held several portfolios. He worked in the Ministry of Tourism, Economics & Civil Aviation, and, went on to be apppointed to head the Ministry of Housing & Social Transformation. In the second term, Minister Baptiste served as the head of the Ministry of Agriculture, Lands, Housing & the Environment, a portfolio he currently holds.

Minister Baptiste is passionate for food and agriculture. During his tenure, he implemented a comprehensive food sustainability programmes which include: Backyard Garden Day, Zero Hunger Initiative, and Pares Youth Program. These are just a few programmes that teach Antiguans & Barbudans different techniques in farming.

Minister Baptiste's many achievements within his Ministry include:

  • Urlings Seafood Fiesta
  • Barbuda Fisheries Complex
  • Follies & North Sound Housing Projects
  • Hosted Caribbean Week of Agriculture 
  • Obtained six scholarships for youth in collaboration with the Guyana School of Agriculture in Guyana
  • Hosted Mangofest on three occasions
  • Restoration of the Botanical Gardens
  • International Workshop on Disaster Risk Management
  • Food & Nutrition Security Plan
  • National Land Use & Zoning Plan

Minister Baptiste pushed a half dozen pieces of legislation dealing with issues including environmental management, fisheries exports to the EU, plant protection, pesticide & toxic chemicals, and the National Physical Development Plan. He's helped facilitate grants from China for agriculture stations equipment and solar powered tools that help reduce energy cost for farmersWhile serving as Minister of Agriculture, Lands, Housing & the Environment, Minister Baptiste was Chairman on the Technical Management Advisory Committee for the Disaster Risk Management Committee under the Jagdeo Initiative.  This was set up to look at mitigation measures to reduce the impact of disasters on the agriculture sector of the Caribbean.

In 2012 he was also the Chairman of the Caribbean Regional Fisheries Mechanism, an organization in Caricom with the mandate to harmonize fishery regulations in order to manage the Caribbean fisheries in a wholelistic way.

Minister Baptiste was charged on two occasions to act as Prime Minister of Antigua & Barbuda by the Hon. Dr. Winston Baldwin Spencer while he was away on Government Business.

He is married to Mrs. Ithlane Baptiste, Principal of the Ottos Comprehensive School. 'Brother B', as he is affectionately known, has been a member of various village community groups.

He has been active advocate on behalf of housing development and other issues facing his constituency. His number one concern:the development of the young people in his constituency.
